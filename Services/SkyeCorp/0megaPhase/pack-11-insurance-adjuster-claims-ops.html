Lane Deliverables

  • Intake lane deliverables

    Branded intake forms: Multi-step forms tuned to your operation so submissions arrive structured, not chaotic.; File uploads: Photos/docs uploads attached to each request for proof and completeness.; Routing rules: Route by service type, urgency, location, or department—no manual shuffling.; Timeline receipts: Every submission gets a timeline: who touched it, what changed, when.; Follow‑up discipline: Missing-info checks and follow-up prompts so leads don’t die in silence.

  • Evidence Vault lane deliverables

    Evidence structure: Claim/case evidence stored in a clean hierarchy.; Versioning: Every evidence update has a receipt.; Timeline receipts: What came in, who reviewed, what changed.; Export packets: Dispute-ready packets exportable.; Sharing controls: Optional client-safe evidence visibility.

  • Dispatch lane deliverables

    Dispatch board: Assignments and status flow for jobs/loads/vendors.; Escalation flags: Overdue and exception markers so issues surface fast.; Proof discipline: Proof attachments where applicable (POD, photos, notes).; Work order exports: Exportable packets for accounting or disputes.; Ops dashboard: Daily throughput view: what’s moving, what’s stuck.

  • Reporting/Proof lane deliverables

    Leadership summaries: Weekly/monthly summaries leadership actually reads.; Proof packs: Timeline + receipts + attachments per case/job/client.; Exports: CSV exports for summary/events and accounting workflows.; Dashboards: Aging, throughput, KPIs tuned to the pack.; Receipt-first standard: Proof is built into the workflow, not bolted on.

  • Client Portal (optional) lane deliverables

    External visibility: Permissioned customer/tenant visibility.; Status-only views: Client-safe status without internal notes.; Secure exchange: Documents exchanged with receipts.; Downloads: Permissioned proof/download buttons.; Reduced inbound: Cuts calls/texts because visibility exists.
