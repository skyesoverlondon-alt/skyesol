Important Details

Tax Implications

Yes, championship payouts are taxable income. If your total payouts exceed $600 in a calendar year, you'll receive a 1099 form from SkyeLetes. We report all payouts to the IRS as required. Plan accordingly—set aside a portion for taxes if needed.

Eligibility Requirements

To receive a championship payout, you must:

  • Be a registered, paid player on the championship roster
  • Have passed height verification
  • Have participated in at least 50% of regular season games
  • Be in good standing with no conduct violations
  • Submit valid payment information within the collection window

Payment Methods

We offer two payout options:

  • Direct Deposit (ACH): Fastest option. Funds typically arrive within 3-5 business days of processing.
  • Check: Mailed to your registered address. Allow 7-10 business days for delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if we win but I missed some games?
You need to have played in at least 50% of regular season games to qualify for championship payouts. This ensures the players who contributed to the championship run are the ones who benefit.
Do bench players get paid too?
Yes. Every rostered, eligible player receives the full $350—regardless of playing time in the championship game. If you're on the roster and you qualified, you get paid.
What about the national championship payout?
The 2026 national championship in Phoenix will have an additional prize pool beyond state payouts. Final amounts will be announced as we approach the event. State champions automatically advance and compete for this additional prize.
Can I decline the payout?
Sure. If you don't submit payment information during the collection window, we'll assume you're declining. The funds don't roll over or transfer to other players.
What if there's a tie or disputed outcome?
Games cannot end in ties—overtime rules apply. Any disputes about eligibility or outcomes are resolved by SkyeLetes management, whose decisions are final.
