The Team Owner Playbook: Building Your Dynasty

Everything you need to know about becoming a SkyeLetes Team Owner—from recruitment strategies to earning potential to leading your squad to championship glory.

You don't just watch basketball. You organize it. You're the one who texts the group chat, books the gym, and makes sure people actually show up. You've got the network, the hustle, and the vision.

You're a natural Team Owner. You just didn't know there was a league built for you.

SkyeLetes Team Ownership isn't about buying a franchise or making a massive investment. It's about leveraging what you already have—connections, credibility, and competitive fire—to build something real. And get paid for it.

What a Team Owner Actually Does

Let's strip away the fancy title and talk about what the role actually involves:

👑 Core Responsibilities

✓ Recruit players — Find 8-15 players under 6'0" who want to compete at a premium level
✓ Build the roster — Share your tracking code so players register under your team
✓ Manage the squad — Be the point of contact for scheduling, communication, and game day
✓ Represent the brand — Work with us during Brand-Lock to design your team's identity
✓ Compete (optional) — Many owners play on their own teams

Notice what's not on that list: paying upfront fees, handling logistics, filming games, or managing complex operations. We handle the infrastructure. You handle the people.

Let's break this down:

  • Player bounties: Every player who registers using your tracking code earns you $50. Recruit a full 15-player roster? That's $750 in bounties alone.
  • Ticket commissions: 15% of ticket sales attributed to your promotional efforts. Your family, friends, and fans buying tickets puts money in your pocket.
  • Champion bonus: If your team wins the state championship, you earn an additional $500. Your players each get $350. Everyone eats.

And remember: there's no upfront cost to become a Team Owner. Your investment is time and hustle, not capital.

Recruitment Strategies That Work

The best Team Owners don't just post on social media and hope. They work their networks strategically. Here are the approaches that actually fill rosters:

🏀

Your Pickup Crew

You already run with people at the park or gym. These are warm leads—they know you, trust you, and want to compete.

🎓

Former Teammates

High school, college, intramural—reach out to guys you used to play with. They're often looking for organized competition.

📱

Local Facebook Groups

Basketball groups, city recreation pages, neighborhood communities. Post about the league with your tracking link.

🏋️

Gym Connections

The guys at your gym who ball. Personal trainers who work with athletes. Basketball training facilities.

The Pitch That Works

When you're recruiting, focus on what makes SkyeLetes different:

  • "It's exclusively for players under 6'0"—no getting bullied by taller guys"
  • "You get custom uniforms and 4K highlight reels—real content you can use"
  • "Win the championship and you get $350 cash"
  • "It's a premium experience—not your typical rec league"

Most players under 6'0" have been waiting their whole lives for something like this. You're not selling—you're offering them exactly what they've wanted.

The Brand-Lock Experience

One of the unique aspects of SkyeLetes is the Brand-Lock session. Once your roster is taking shape, you'll work with our design team to create your team's identity:

  • Team name: Your choice (within professional guidelines)
  • Colors: Primary and secondary palette
  • Uniform design: Jersey and shorts customization
  • Team branding: Logo elements and styling

This isn't generic gear. Your team will look professional, cohesive, and distinctive. The pride players feel putting on those uniforms? That's part of the premium experience you're offering.

Playing and Owning

Here's a question we get constantly: "Can I play on my own team?"

Absolutely.

Many of our best Team Owners are also players. They recruit their squad, lead from the court, and compete for championships alongside their roster. You just need to:

  • Meet the height requirement (under 6'0" barefoot)
  • Complete player registration and pay your player fee
  • Be roster-active (no bench-only ownership)

As a playing owner, you earn bounties for recruitment and compete for player payouts. Win the championship and you get the $500 owner bonus plus your $350 player payout. That's $850 in your pocket, plus all the bounties and commissions.

What We Provide

  • Payment processing: We handle all transactions and payouts
  • Uniform production: Design session through delivery
  • Venue coordination: Game scheduling and facility booking
  • Media production: 4K filming, highlight creation, content distribution
  • Championship structure: Standings, playoffs, and finals organization
  • Owner dashboard: Track your roster, bounties, and commissions in real-time

You focus on people. We focus on everything else.
