In a real emergency, memory gets unreliable. Names, dosages, allergies, vaccine dates— the exact information you need becomes the hardest thing to recall. A medical passport solves one problem: make the essentials easy to retrieve.

Why This Exists

Most families don’t lack information. They lack fast access in a format that’s usable under stress. When care is urgent, speed and clarity matter more than perfection.

The failure mode is predictable: a child gets sick, a parent gets injured, an elder has a reaction, and suddenly you’re answering questions while juggling worry, noise, and time pressure. FamilyMed Passport is built to reduce that chaos.

Preparedness is removing guesswork from moments that punish guesswork.
— A simple rule: reduce cognitive load

What FamilyMed Passport Does

One record per person. Clear sections. A clean export when you need something shareable.

The flow is intentionally simple: create a household member, fill the essentials in separated sections, use Emergency Mode when you need fast access, and export a clean PDF for printing or sharing.

Overview

Identity essentials and context that helps anyone quickly understand who the record is for.

Medical

Conditions, allergies, medications, and “do not” notes to reduce avoidable mistakes.

Vaccines

A single place for vaccine history so you’re not searching portals when time is tight.

Notes

Caregiver handoffs, prior reactions, instructions, and anything you never want to “re-remember.”

The 7-Minute Setup Order

Speed comes from sequence. Here’s the sequence.

  • Minute 1: Add your first household member

    Start with the person most likely to need urgent access (kids, elders, chronic conditions).

  • Minutes 2–3: Medical essentials only

    Allergies, meds, conditions. Capture what prevents mistakes first.

  • Minute 4: Vaccine highlights

    Enough to answer common “is this up to date?” questions quickly.

  • Minute 5: Notes for caregiver handoff

    Prior reactions, instructions, or “what happened last time” details you’ll want later.

  • Minutes 6–7: Export the PDF

    Export and store a copy where it’s reachable when life gets loud.

The right information in the right shape at the right time changes outcomes.
— Design constraint: emergency retrieval

FAQ

Is this medical advice?

No. This is an organizational tool. Always follow licensed medical guidance and local emergency procedures.

What should I put in first?

Allergies, medications (name + dose if known), major conditions, and anything a clinician needs to avoid harm.

Can I print this?

Yes. Use “Print Kit to PDF,” and use the app’s export feature for a clean, shareable medical passport PDF.

The 7-Minute Emergency Setup Kit

This is an organizational tool, not medical advice. In emergencies call local emergency services (911 in the U.S.).

Minute-by-Minute Setup

  1. Add Member (start with highest-risk)
  2. Medical essentials: allergies, meds, conditions
  3. Vaccines: key dates
  4. Notes: caregiver handoff + prior reactions + instructions
  5. Export PDF: store where you can reach it fast

Emergency Snapshot Prompts

  • Allergies (food/med/environment)
  • Current meds + dosing notes
  • Major conditions / devices
  • Physician / clinic contacts
  • Insurance basics (if relevant)
  • Caregiver instructions (“if X, do Y”)
