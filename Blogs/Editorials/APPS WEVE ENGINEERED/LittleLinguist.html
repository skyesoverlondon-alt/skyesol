Why This Works (Even When Life Gets Busy)

Most language learning stalls for one simple reason: practice isn’t predictable.

This kit uses a three-part micro-routine that fits into ordinary days. Each part has a different purpose: get exposure, build speaking comfort, and strengthen recall. Tiny sessions add up fast because they actually happen.

1) Exposure

See and hear today’s words. No pressure — you’re building familiarity.

2) Speaking

Say the words out loud. Confidence grows when the mouth practices, not just the eyes.

3) Recall

Try to remember without looking. That effort is what makes learning “stick.”

A tiny 1-line log

Write one line per day: “Words used today: ____”. It’s simple and surprisingly motivating.

Make practice small. Make practice steady. That’s how confidence shows up — quietly, then all at once.

FAQ

Quick answers for parents, teachers, and learners.

What if attention is short?

Use the 6-minute option. Two minutes at a time works incredibly well — especially with the prompt pack. The goal is “repeatable,” not “perfect.”

Do we need to print anything?

Printing is optional. The flashcards are helpful for quick recall drills, but you can also practice directly in Little Linguist.

How soon will we see progress?

Most people notice confidence first: quicker responses, less hesitation, and more willingness to speak. The routine is built to make those wins visible in a week.

Can teachers use this in class?

Yes. The structure works for warm-ups, stations, tutoring sessions, and homework routines. The prompts make it easy to keep everyone engaged.
