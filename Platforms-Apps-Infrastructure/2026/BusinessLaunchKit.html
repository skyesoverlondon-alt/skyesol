The Problem

New businesses spend 3 months building infrastructure before they ever serve a client

Writing contracts from scratch, building intake forms one by one, pricing from gut feeling, onboarding winging it every time — operator time lost to setup is money left on the table.

📄

The Blank Document Problem

Every new business owner stares at a blank contract template, a blank questionnaire, a blank rate card. Building from scratch is slow, error-prone, and keeps you from the revenue-generating work you actually launched to do.

🎲

Pricing Guesswork

Underpricing is the #1 reason new service businesses fail in the first year. Without a structured pricing architecture — tiered packages, rate cards, scope definitions — operators lose margin on every job while convincing themselves they're being competitive.

🔄

No Consistent Onboarding

When every client onboarding is improvised, the experience is inconsistent, important information gets missed, and the business looks amateur. A structured onboarding workflow signals professionalism, sets expectations, and reduces refund and dispute rates significantly.

⚖️

Legal Exposure

Operating without a proper service agreement, scope of work, or payment terms document is how operators lose money to scope creep, non-payment, and disputes. BusinessLaunchKit provides attorney-reviewed templates that protect your time and your income from the first client forward.

What's Inside

47 assets. Every system a new business needs. Nothing you have to build from scratch.

BusinessLaunchKit is organized into six core sections — brand, legal, intake, pricing, onboarding, and launch — each containing ready-to-edit templates built for service-based businesses.

🏷️

Brand Identity Starter

Business name brief, brand positioning statement, target audience profile, color palette guide (primary + 2 accent), typography recommendation pair, brand voice guide (tone, vocabulary, examples), and a logo brief for working with a designer or AI tool. Everything to establish a coherent identity on day one.

📋

Legal Templates

Six core legal documents: general service agreement, project scope of work, client NDA, independent contractor agreement, payment terms addendum, and a project change order form. All written in plain English with clearly marked customization fields — fill in your business name and services, and they're ready for use.

📝

Client Intake System

Five intake forms covering every common service engagement: new client discovery questionnaire, project brief form, service intake (recurring clients), emergency/rush project intake, and a client re-engagement form. Each form is designed to collect the information you actually need to scope, price, and deliver the work without three follow-up calls.

💰

Pricing Architecture

A complete pricing framework: three-tier service package template (starter / standard / premium), hourly rate calculator worksheet, project flat-rate scoping guide, and a client-facing rate card. Includes a pricing methodology explainer that walks through how to calculate your minimum viable rate based on target income and billable hours.

🤝

Client Onboarding Workflow

Seven onboarding assets: welcome email template, client portal setup guide, kickoff meeting agenda, project timeline template, communication preferences form, first invoice template, and a 30-day check-in touchpoint script. Delivers a structured, professional first impression with every new client from the moment they sign.

🚀

60-Task Launch Checklist

A week-by-week launch checklist covering every critical setup task for a new service business — business registration research, banking setup, website essentials, social profile creation, first offer definition, outreach sequence setup, and first client acquisition steps. Organized by priority so you do the high-impact tasks first.

📣

First Offer Framework

A structured template for defining and positioning your first productized offer — including pricing, deliverable scope, what's included vs. excluded, turnaround time, and the positioning statement for your website, proposals, and outreach. Built around the principle of one clear offer for one clearly defined client, launched fast.

📊

Business Metrics Starter

A simple monthly metrics tracker — revenue, average project value, client count, referral sources, cost of service, and profit margin. Not a complex accounting system — a lightweight monthly check-in template that keeps you oriented to what the numbers are actually saying about the health of the business.

How It Works

Download → Customize → Operate → Launch

Four steps from kit download to first client served.

01
Download

Access the full kit — 47 assets in one organized folder, ready to customize

Open the BusinessLaunchKit and download the full package — all 47 assets organized into six clearly labeled folders: Brand, Legal, Intake, Pricing, Onboarding, and Launch. Everything is provided in editable formats (Google Docs, Notion, PDF) so you can work in whatever environment you're already in.

02
Customize

Fill in your business details — most templates take 15–30 minutes each

Each template has clearly marked fields to replace — your business name, service description, pricing, contact details, and any specifics relevant to your industry. Most operators complete the six core documents (service agreement, intake form, rate card, welcome email, kickoff agenda, metrics tracker) in a single afternoon sitting. The kit is designed to be filled in fast, not edited forever.

03
Operate

Use the systems with every client — intake, contracts, onboarding, tracking

Start using the kit immediately with your first client. Send the intake form before the discovery call. Use the service agreement for every engagement. Run the kickoff meeting from the agenda. Send the welcome email on day one. Track monthly metrics in the worksheet. The systems compound — the longer you use them, the more professional and consistent your client experience becomes.

04
Launch

Work the 60-task checklist — hit every setup milestone in order, fast

The 60-task launch checklist sequences every setup action by priority and week — ensuring you get the high-leverage tasks done first (offer definition, client system setup, outreach prep) before moving to the secondary tasks (social presence, email signature, review request process). By the end of the checklist, you have a fully operational business infrastructure ready for your first real client.
