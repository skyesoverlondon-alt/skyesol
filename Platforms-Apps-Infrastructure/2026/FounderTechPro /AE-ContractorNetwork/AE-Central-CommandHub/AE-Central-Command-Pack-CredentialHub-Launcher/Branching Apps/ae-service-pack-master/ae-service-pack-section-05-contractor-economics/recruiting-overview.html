What you are actually selling

The easiest mistake a new contractor can make is thinking this is one service. It is a stacked offer library.

Authority lane

Web builds, executive builds, executive authority, and Beacon give the contractor a way to sell trust, lead capture, ranking posture, local visibility, and authority compounding.

Pages to know: CineFrame, Executive Web Builds, Executive Authority, Skye Ops Beacon.

Revenue lane

CheckoutForge, BrandForge, PulseWire, and ContentEngine let the contractor sell how the buyer gets found, converts, follows up, and improves brand trust.

Pages to know: CheckoutForge, BrandForge, PulseWire, ContentEngine.

Operator lane

DataPilot, AccessAtlas, SignalFlow, TrustLayer, ConnectBridge, VaultOps, and InsightForge are for buyers who need governed systems, routing, dashboards, and operational control.

Pages to know: DataPilot, AccessAtlas, SignalFlow, TrustLayer, ConnectBridge, VaultOps, InsightForge.

Stability lane

MigrateOps, ShieldStack, RetainEngine, and LaunchPad are for buyers who need modernization, hardening, retention, or an MVP in market fast.

Pages to know: MigrateOps, ShieldStack, RetainEngine, LaunchPad.

How contractors make money inside this library

The money is not one-dimensional. There are three distinct lanes in the comp model used throughout this project.
Project Money

Deposits

Your fast money comes from collected deposits. Most public service pages use a 30% deposit structure. Executive Web / Authority and Beacon setup use 25% instead.

Internal comp model used here: 46% of the collected deposit.

Long-Tail Money

Recurring Revenue

Beacon, kAIxU platform plans, and recurring retainers give the contractor a path that continues after the first project-side payout.

Internal comp model used here: 60% of year-one recurring revenue.

Usage Money

AI Usage Uplift

When a contractor lands kAIxU usage, usage can become a separate earning lane on top of the plan itself.

Internal comp model used here: 31% of client AI usage.

How to explain the contractor path simply

This is the version a recruit should understand in one reading.

You come in as an independent contractor working on your own time. You sell from a real service library, not a vague “marketing package.” Most deals begin with a deposit, which is where your upfront money is modeled from. Then some accounts continue into monthly revenue through Beacon, platform plans, or add-on retainers. If you prove yourself over time or by closing six cases, you can become eligible for an office role with base hourly pay plus commission.
