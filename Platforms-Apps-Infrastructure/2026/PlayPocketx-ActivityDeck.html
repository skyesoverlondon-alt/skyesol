The Problem

Facilitators shouldn't have to Google "icebreaker ideas" five minutes before a meeting

🔍

Activity Hunting Kills Momentum

Searching for the right icebreaker mid-prep means scrolling through blog posts, Pinterest boards, and YouTube videos and still not finding something that fits your group. PlayPocketx curates and delivers a ready-to-run card in one tap.

📶

Wi-Fi Dependency at the Worst Times

Workshops happen in conference rooms with spotty Wi-Fi, retreat centers with no internet, and client offices where you're on a guest network that blocks apps. PlayPocketx installs as a PWA and runs fully offline — no connection needed after the first load.

⏱️

Activities Without Structure Don't Land

A good activity prompt without facilitation instructions, timing guidance, and group-size context often falls flat. PlayPocketx cards include the prompt, how-to-facilitate notes, suggested timing, and energy level — everything to run it confidently.

🔁

No Memory of What Worked Before

Facilitators who run the same group repeatedly lose track of which activities landed and which fell flat. PlayPocketx session history and favorites let you build a personal library of what works for your team — without a spreadsheet.

Features

80+ ready-to-run activities. One tap. No prep required.

🃏

4 Curated Decks

Icebreaker, Brainstorm, Retrospective, and Warm-Up decks — each card is a complete activity with prompt, facilitation notes, timing, and group-size recommendation. No searching, just drawing.

📶

Offline-First PWA

Install PlayPocketx once and it runs fully offline. Service worker caches all 80+ cards and their full activity content — Wi-Fi dead zones, retreat centers, and conference rooms with locked networks don't slow you down.

Built-In Activity Timer

Each card has a suggested time range. Tap "Start Timer" and a visible countdown runs on screen — visible to the whole room if you're projecting. Soft end-of-time alert so you can wrap up cleanly.

Before drawing, set filters for group size (2–5, 6–15, 16+), energy level (Low / Medium / High), and time available (5 min, 10 min, 15 min) — PlayPocketx only draws cards that fit your constraints.

Star any card to add it to Favorites. After each session, your draw history is saved so you know exactly what you ran, when, and can replay a crowd favorite in seconds for a different group.

Shuffle the entire deck and draw randomly for maximum variety, or browse the deck and hand-pick a card by category — PlayPocketx supports both show-me-what-to-run and I-know-what-I-want workflows.

📋

Facilitation Briefs

Every card includes a facilitator brief — the prompt to read aloud, step-by-step run instructions, common stumbling points to watch for, and a debrief question to close out the activity with intention.

How It Works

Install → Filter → Draw → Run

01
Install

Open PlayPocketx in your browser and install it as a PWA from the prompt

On first visit, your browser shows an "Add to Home Screen" install prompt. Tap it and PlayPocketx installs as a native-like app — it appears on your home screen, opens full-screen, and all 80+ cards are cached offline immediately.

02
Filter

Set your filters — group size, energy level, and time available

Before drawing, use the three filter controls to narrow the deck to cards that fit your situation. Running a 20-person all-hands with 5 minutes to spare and need medium energy? Set those filters and only the right cards will draw.

03
Draw

Tap "Draw a Card" — get the prompt, facilitation brief, and timer in one screen

The drawn card fills the screen with everything you need: the activity name and deck type, the exact prompt to read aloud, step-by-step facilitation notes, and the activity timer pre-set to the suggested duration. No scrolling required — it's all visible at once.

04
Run

Start the timer, run the activity, and save it to favorites if it lands

Tap "Start Timer" to begin. Run the activity. After completion, the debrief question prompts you to close with intention. Then choose: star it as a favorite, draw another card, or view your session history to log what you ran today.
