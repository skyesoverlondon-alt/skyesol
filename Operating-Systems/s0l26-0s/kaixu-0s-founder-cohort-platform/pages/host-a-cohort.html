How this works

An open invitation with a real approval gate.

Organizations can request a cohort. That request is then reviewed for mission alignment, participant readiness, facility support, scheduling, and whether the organization is positioned to make the cohort valuable for the people it serves.

Step 1

Submit an organization request.

Use the form below to describe your organization, who the cohort would serve, your preferred timeline, and what kind of support or structure you want from the SOLEnterprise side.

Step 2

We vet the organization.

We review mission fit, seriousness, contact quality, space readiness, participant profile, and whether your environment matches the standards required for a founder-led AI and platform-build cohort.

Step 3

Approval remains discretionary.

Acceptance is subject to approval by SOLEnterprise (International Nexus & Holdings). Some requests may move forward quickly. Others may be deferred, reshaped, or declined.

Best-fit environments

The strongest cohort requests usually come from organizations with real participant access and a place to build.

Resource rooms, learning labs, youth centers, nonprofit education programs, re-entry pathways, workforce-development groups, community innovation programs, and mission-aligned institutional partners are all strong candidates when the organization is serious and the environment can support a live, hands-on build week.
