Snapshot
Client & Situation

Profile

  • Arizona-based services operator with both in-city and remote delivery.
  • Multiple sales channels (direct invoices, platform payouts, card payments).
  • Had economic nexus risk in Surprise without an active local TPT license.

Problem

Needed a Surprise TPT license and catch-up reporting without triggering penalties. Historic sales were fragmented across payment platforms with inconsistent location coding.

Approach
What We Delivered

Nexus Map

Mapped city vs. state nexus, isolated Surprise-sourced receipts, and excluded out-of-city deliveries to avoid over-reporting.

Data Reconciliation

Consolidated sales from invoicing, POS, and platform payouts; normalized date and jurisdiction tags to align with TPT return requirements.

Filing Runbook

Sequenced license application, account activation, and back-period filings with reviewer-ready schedules and payment proofs.

Timeline
21-Day Run to Activation

Week 1

  • Day 1–2: Nexus and location mapping; gathered platform exports and bank traces.
  • Day 3–4: Cleaned transaction data; labeled Surprise-sourced receipts; drafted taxability matrix.
  • Day 5: Submitted Surprise TPT application with supporting schedules.

Week 2–3

  • Day 9: Account number issued; portal access activated.
  • Day 12–15: Filed back-period returns with reconciled receipts and payment proofs.
  • Day 21: License active; confirmations received—no penalties assessed.

Playbook
How We De-Risked the Filing

Controls

  • Segmented receipts by service location to exclude non-Surprise deliveries.
  • Cross-walked platform fee statements to gross receipts to prevent under-reporting.
  • Bound taxability positions to published MCTC guidance for services.
  • Attached payment proofs and bank traces to each back-period return.

Artifacts Delivered

  • Nexus map and taxability matrix (city vs. state scope).
  • Reconciled receipt schedules by month and channel.
  • Filing packet with license application, portal setup, and confirmation receipts.
  • Replicable runbook for future filings and audits.
