Snapshot
Client & Situation

Profile

  • Owner-operated professional services firm (regulated support practice).
  • Clients served remotely; periodic compliance checks only.
  • Residence in a Phoenix zoning district requiring home occupation clearance.

Problem

The client needed to keep serving customers while satisfying zoning, fire prevention, and business licensing requirements—without triggering inspections that could stall revenue or demand costly retrofits.

Approach
What We Delivered

Regulatory Map

Mapped the exact approvals: zoning home occupation clearance, fire prevention review, and the linked city business license. Sequenced tasks to avoid duplicate inspections.

Application Packet

Prepared site diagram, use narrative, parking impact, egress description, and hazardous materials attestation tailored to the declared use.

Agency Coordination

Filed, tracked, and responded to reviewers; aligned fire and zoning on the same declared use to prevent conflicting conditions.

Timeline
16-Day Run to Approval

Week 1

  • Day 1–2: Intake, site walk, zoning cross-check, fire code applicability.
  • Day 3–4: Drafted site plan, parking/no-traffic statement, and use narrative.
  • Day 5: Submitted home occupation packet; routed fire review concurrently.

Week 2–3

  • Day 8: Reviewer questions cleared (home office only, no onsite clients).
  • Day 12: Fire prevention cleared with no inspection required.
  • Day 16: Home occupation approval issued; business license released same day.
Evidence & Outcomes
Results That Withstand Scrutiny
0
Inspection findings

Cleared fire prevention without onsite inspection; narrative and photos satisfied review.

16
Days to approval

Calendar days from kick-off to issued home occupation and business license.

3
Agencies aligned

Zoning, fire prevention, and licensing kept on a single declared-use track.

Playbook
How We De-Risked the Filing

Controls

  • Declared “no client visits” and “no storage of regulated materials” to avoid fire inspection triggers.
  • Documented parking impact as zero additional trips; attached street-view context.
  • Provided ingress/egress description showing no changes to egress paths.
  • Bound use description to NAICS code and matched it across filings.

Playbook
How We De-Risked the Filing

Controls

  • Declared “no client visits” and “no storage of regulated materials” to avoid fire inspection triggers.

  • Bound use description to NAICS code and matched it across filings.

Artifacts Delivered

  • Use narrative and occupant load statement.
  • Scaled site sketch with parking and entry points.
  • Fire prevention attestation (no hazardous storage, no open flame processes).
  • Cross-referenced packet for zoning, fire, and license submissions.
