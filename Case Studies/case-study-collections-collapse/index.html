Snapshot
Client & Situation

Profile

  • Regional services firm with recurring invoices and card-on-file billing.
  • Recent system migration broke dunning workflows and ledger sync.
  • Collections collapsed; aging doubled in 45 days; CSAT at risk.

Problem

Failed automations, mismatched balances across CRM, billing, and bank deposits, and no working contact sequences. Risk of charge-offs and brand damage if handled bluntly.

Approach
What We Delivered

Ledger Rebuild

Reconciled billing system, CRM, and bank traces into a single source of truth with customer-level schedules.

Sequenced Outreach

Designed multi-channel dunning (email/SMS/voice) with tone tiers to preserve CSAT while restoring cash flow.

Controls & QA

Put guardrails on retries, fee assessments, and promise-to-pay tracking; added QA checks before postings.

Timeline
30-Day Stabilization

Week 1

  • Day 1–2: Freeze failed automations; export CRM/billing; pull bank statements.
  • Day 3–4: Rebuild customer balances; flag disputes and duplicates.
  • Day 5: Publish single ledger; launch manual outreach with QA checklist.

Week 2–4

  • Day 10: New sequenced dunning live (email/SMS/voice) with tone tiers.
  • Day 18: Promise-to-pay tracker live; fee policy aligned to terms.
  • Day 30: Automations reinstated with controls; aging curve trending down.

Playbook
How We De-Risked the Recovery

Controls

  • Locked fee/interest until balances were validated to avoid disputes.
  • Applied QA review before any automated retry or balance post.
  • Segmented customers by risk and tenure to tailor messaging.
  • Escalation path with human review for disputes and hardship cases.

Artifacts Delivered

  • Unified customer ledger with bank trace reconciliation.
  • Dunning templates (email/SMS/voice) with tone tiers and QA checks.
  • Promise-to-pay tracker with monitoring dashboard.
  • Automation runbook with rollback plan and guardrails.
